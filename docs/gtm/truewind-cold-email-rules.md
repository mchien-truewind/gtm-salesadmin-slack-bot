# Truewind Cold Email Rules

## Personalization

- Do not paste the recipient's job title into the email as a template phrase.
- Use the job title to infer the recipient's daily pain, responsibilities, and decision pressure.
- For finance leaders, write to the work they own: close quality, review burden, reconciliations, accrual support, exceptions, sign-off, reporting confidence, and team time.
- The recipient should feel that Truewind understands the work behind their role, not that their title was inserted into copy.

## Grammar

- Cold emails must use full, grammatically correct sentences.
- Do not use sentence fragments, noun-list paragraphs, or compressed shorthand.
- If a line would not read clearly to a cold recipient on first pass, rewrite it.

## CTA

- Keep the CTA simple, direct, and concrete.
- Avoid passive CTAs such as "would it help" when asking for a meeting.
- The CTA must make clear what the recipient gets and how much time is required.
